## 1. Liner Installation

### Core Competencies
- Site preparation – grade, base, and no hidden rocks.
- Liner selection – size, thickness, pattern, bead type.
- Placement and fitting – no wrinkles, no excuses.
- Bead lock and coping – torque specs matter.
- Vacuuming and final adjust – get the air out before the water goes in.

### Step‑by‑Step (The Merlin Way)

**1. Prepare the Pool Shell**  
- Check every wall. If there’s a screw poking through, fix it. If there’s a gap in the bracing, shore it up. This is your foundation; if it’s wrong, the liner’s wrong.  
- Run your hand along the cove – should be smooth, no hard edges. Use foam if you have to. This isn’t a Benny’s weekend project.

**2. Measure and Order the Liner**  
- Measure length, width, depth, corner radii – twice. Closure error over one inch and you’re ordering a replacement on your own dime.  
- Account for overlap based on bead type. Check the manufacturer’s specs. If you guess, you’re guessing wrong.

**3. Unfold and Position**  
- Lay the liner in the sun to warm it up – cold vinyl fights you. Center it over the pool like you’re lining up a runway approach.  
- Align seams with the pool shape. If it’s a Grecian, those seams better hit the corners dead on.

**4. Seat the Bead**  
- Start at one corner, press that bead into the track. Use a rubber mallet – gently, you’re not pounding a stake.  
- Work your way around, pulling out wrinkles as you go. If you get a bubble, stop, lift, reseat. Do it right the first time.

**5. Attach Faceplates and Skimmers**  
- Cut openings carefully – measure twice, cut once. Use a sharp utility knife, not your pocket knife.  
- Install faceplates with gaskets. Torque the screws evenly – don’t overdo it, you’ll strip the plastic.

**6. Vacuum and Fill**  
- Place a shop vac over a skimmer hole, seal it with tape. Pull the liner tight against the walls.  
- Start filling while you smooth out remaining wrinkles. Keep an eye on water level – if it drops, the liner shifts.

### Safety & Quality
- Never drag liner over rough ground – use a tarp.  
- Use a soft broom to smooth, not a metal rake.  
- Check bead seating before filling – if it pops out later, you’re draining.  
- Fill slowly; rushing causes wrinkles.

## 2. Liner Measurement

### Core Competencies
- Linear dimensions – length, width, depth – with confidence.  
- Irregular shapes – kidney, offset, freeform – break them into triangles.  
- Corner radii – if you can’t measure it, you can’t order it.  
- Calculating liner size – formulas are your friend.

### Tools of the Trade
- 100‑ft tape measure (the good one, not the rusty one).  
- Laser distance measurer – for when the tape won’t reach.  
- Angle finder for radii – or a framing square and some geometry.  
- A notepad, not your phone – phones die, paper doesn’t.

### Procedure (The A‑B Triangulation Method)

**1. Sketch the Pool**  
- Draw it freehand, but make it accurate. Mark every wall, step, bench, light.  
- If it’s a kidney, draw the centerline and measure offsets every three feet.

**2. Measure Length and Width**  
- For rectangles: measure at both ends and the middle. If they don’t match, the wall’s out of square – fix it or note it.  
- For freeform: run a baseline, measure perpendicular offsets. This ain’t art; it’s math.

**3. Measure Depth**  
- Shallow end, deep end, slope. Record depth at walls and at every change.  
- If there’s a hopper bottom, measure the flat and the slopes separately.

**4. Capture Corner Radii**  
- Use an angle finder or a radius gauge. If you don’t have one, trace the corner on cardboard and measure the curve.  
- Radius under one foot? That’s a wrinkling risk – warn the customer.

**5. Account for Overlap**  
- Add the required overlap – usually three to six inches. Check the bead type.  
- Don’t forget the cove – if you’re using foam, add its thickness.

**6. Double‑Check**  
- Re‑measure critical dimensions. If possible, have another person verify.  
- Calculate closure error – the difference between your last point and the first. Over one inch? Start over.

### Common Pitfalls
- Forgetting the cove thickness.  
- Misreading tape on curved walls – use a flexible tape.  
- Ignoring obstructions – ladders, steps, lights.  
- Trusting a Benny’s “eyeball” estimate.

## 3. Liner Repairs

### Core Competencies
- Identifying damage – tear, puncture, seam separation, bead failure.  
- Underwater vs. drained repair – know which you’re doing.  
- Patching materials – vinyl, adhesive, primer.  
- Seam sealing – don’t make it worse.

### Types of Damage and Fixes

| Damage Type | Repair Method |
|-------------|---------------|
| Small puncture (<1 inch) | Underwater patch kit – clean, cut patch round, apply adhesive, hold. |
| Larger tear (>1 inch) | Drain below damage, clean, dry, cut patch oversized, use vinyl cement, roll out air. |
| Seam separation | Clean area, apply seam sealer, clamp overnight. |
| Bead lock failure | Re‑seat bead or replace coping strip – don’t jury‑rig it. |

### Underwater Repair (When Draining Isn’t an Option)
1. Scrub the area with a pool brush – get the algae off.  
2. Cut a patch with rounded corners – square corners peel.  
3. Apply underwater adhesive to the patch.  
4. Press firmly, hold for 30 seconds, smooth edges.  
5. It’ll hold, but keep an eye on it.

### Drained Repair (The Right Way)
1. Drain water below the damage.  
2. Clean and dry thoroughly – use a heat gun if you have to.  
3. Cut patch at least two inches larger than the tear.  
4. Apply vinyl cement to both surfaces, let it tack up.  
5. Press patch, use a roller to remove bubbles.  
6. Wait 24 hours before refilling – no shortcuts.

### Safety & Longevity
- Use only materials rated for pool liners – no duct tape, no silicone caulk.  
- Avoid patches near high‑stress areas – corners, steps – if possible.  
- After refill, check the patch for a few days. If it lifts, you did it wrong.

## 4. Basic Pool Care

### Core Competencies
- Water chemistry – pH, chlorine, alkalinity, calcium hardness.  
- Routine cleaning – skimming, brushing, vacuuming.  
- Filter maintenance – cartridge, sand, DE.  
- Seasonal procedures – opening, closing, winterizing.

### Daily/Weekly Tasks
- **Skim the surface** – leaves and bugs become algae food.  
- **Brush walls and floor** – especially corners and steps. Algae loves a lazy pool.  
- **Check water level** – keep it mid‑skimmer. Too low, pump runs dry; too high, skimmer doesn’t work.  
- **Empty skimmer and pump baskets** – clogged baskets reduce flow.

### Chemical Balance Guidelines
| Parameter | Ideal Range | Neil’s Note |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Free Chlorine | 1–3 ppm | Below 1, you’re growing a swamp. Above 3, you’re burning eyes. |
| pH | 7.4–7.6 | Low pH eats equipment; high pH scales up. |
| Total Alkalinity | 80–120 ppm | Buffers pH swings. Keep it in line. |
| Calcium Hardness | 200–400 ppm | Too low, water gets aggressive; too high, scale. |
| Cyanuric Acid | 30–50 ppm | Stabilizer – too much locks up chlorine. |

### Seasonal Care
- **Spring Opening**: Remove cover, clean debris, shock, balance, run filter. Check for damage over winter.  
- **Summer**: Run pump 8–12 hours daily, test weekly, brush often.  
- **Fall/Winter**: Lower water level, blow out lines, install winter cover. Don’t forget the gizmo in the skimmer.

### Troubleshooting Common Issues
- **Green water** – Shock treatment, brush, run filter continuously. If it’s still green after two days, check your phosphates.  
- **Cloudy water** – Filter might be clogged, or your chemistry is off. Check and balance, then add clarifier.  
- **Algae spots** – Brush vigorously, apply algaecide, increase chlorine. If it’s black algae, good luck – you’ll be scrubbing for days.  
- **Stains** – Metal stains need ascorbic acid; organic stains need chlorine. Test first.

## Appendix: Essential Formulas

- **Rectangular pool volume (gallons)** = length (ft) × width (ft) × average depth (ft) × 7.5  
- **Circular pool volume** = diameter² × average depth × 5.9  
- **Kidney pool volume** = (longest length × widest width × average depth × 7.0) – close enough.  
- **Liner size for rectangle** = (length + 2×overlap) × (width + 2×overlap)  
- **Closure error** = |measured length around perimeter – calculated length| – keep it under 1 inch.

*Always verify with manufacturer specs – they don’t care about your math if it’s wrong.*
